Overview

Purpose

For over a year now, I’ve been running a Minecraft server for friends and family. We’ve had many fun worlds, all of which have unique memories. There is an issue, however: everyone wants access to these world files at random points in time, but the files are too massive to easily transfer them around. How can I make these Minecraft worlds easily distributable? A website. I would like to create a website that would have download links for said files. This would allow my loved ones to go to a URL and download the world files without having to contact me, thus having a reliable and frictionless way to easily access their memories. Additionally, I will be able to have a portfolio page to show potential employers my proudest projects.

Audience

The main audience for this website would be my fellow Minecraft players. They would be able to use this site to download prized worlds, which is something I've not been able to give them before. Another audience would be future employers. I intend to use this site itself as a passion project, as well as have a page to act as my portfolio. Lastly, a small portion of my visitors will be family and friends that I simply want to show my work to. I want this page to be something I'm truly proud of!

Dodododo World Downloads

There will be a header to go back to the main page, to my github, or to my portfolio. The header will also have the “F” logo. There needs to be a paragraph that explains to the users how to download and modify Minecraft worlds that have been modified by Paper. This is because Paper saved Nether and End data differently than vanilla Minecraft. This paragraph could look something like this:

“Important information about the world downloads: These worlds ran on Paper Minecraft (a very efficient server jar that we’ve used for awhile). This means that the Nether and End dimension data has been moved to a location that Vanilla Minecraft will not recognize. With that being said, this is fairly easy to fix. First, you will want to extract the zip file (you can do this in your downloads folder). Then go into the newly extracted folder. You should see, among other things, two files that look like “[world name]_the_nether” and “[world name]_the_end”. Simply rename “[worldname]_the_end” to “DIM1”, and rename “[worldname]_the_nether” to “DIM-1”. Finally, move both the newly named “DIM1” and “DIM-1” files inside the plain “[worldname]” folder.

You have now successfully reformatted the world downloads, and you can now drop the entire folder (not the subfolders, but the folder that was created when you extracted the zip) into your .minecraft/saves folder. If you don’t know how to do this, there is a handy guide HERE. Additionally, you may have to scroll down on your Minecraft single player client before you see the world download.” Important quotes (such as the file names) need to be highlighted or somehow distinguished (similar to how ```some code``` will appear differently on many websites). This section should appear near the top of the screen. Each world download will have its own small section. This section will be divided in half vertically. On one side will be a screenshot from the world, and on the other side will have a short description of the world, as well as a few significant details such as the world file name or the download size. After the world downloads, there will be a section with contact instructions. This will include my discord username, as well as my Kopaunik Software email address.

Personal Portfolio

The portfolio page will have the same header as the download page (buttons to go back to the main page, to my github, or to my portfolio. The header will also have the “F” logo). Then there needs to be a body of text explaining that the page is to show some of my proudest work, as well as work that shows skills in diverse areas. These projects will be shown in the form of an inline video. The text will describe what the project is, as well as why it is significant enough to put on this page. Meanwhile the video (which will NOT autoplay) will show a demo of some sort for the project. The video will be hosted on YouTube. There may also be a section with other resume details (education, languages, etc). This would allow my portfolio to end with a reminder of my resume. This will mainly be to impress future employers. The footer will have a section with contact instructions. These will be different from the downloads page. This will have my formal email address (JoshuaKopaunik@gmail.com).
